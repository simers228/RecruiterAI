Job Title: Construction Assistant Superintendent

Company: DEF General Subcontractor

Description:
DEF General Subcontractor is looking for a dedicated and skilled Construction Assistant Superintendent to support our construction management team. The ideal candidate will work closely with the General Superintendent to ensure our projects are completed safely, on time, and with exceptional quality.

Responsibilities:

Support the General Superintendent in overseeing daily on-site construction operations, ensuring work is completed according to project specifications and schedules.
Assist in coordinating construction crews, subcontractors, and other on-site personnel, providing guidance and direction as needed.
Help develop, implement, and monitor project schedules, making adjustments as required to meet changing conditions and deadlines.
Collaborate with project managers, clients, architects, and other project stakeholders to address project-related issues and ensure smooth project execution.
Ensure compliance with health and safety regulations, company policies, and industry standards.
Regularly inspect work in progress to maintain quality control and identify potential issues or areas for improvement.
Keep accurate and up-to-date project documentation, such as daily logs, progress reports, and safety records.
Requirements:

High school diploma or equivalent; further education in construction management, engineering, or a related field is preferred.
Minimum of 3 years of experience in the construction industry, with some supervisory or leadership experience.
Knowledge of construction processes, materials, and safety regulations.
Ability to read and interpret construction drawings, specifications, and contracts.
Strong communication, organizational, and teamwork skills.
Able to work under pressure and adapt to changing priorities and deadlines.
Valid driver's license and willingness to travel to project sites as needed.
Optional Requirements:

Certification or training in construction safety, such as OSHA 10-hour certification.
Experience in specific construction sectors, such as residential, commercial, or infrastructure projects.
Bilingual or multilingual abilities, particularly in languages relevant to the local construction workforce.
