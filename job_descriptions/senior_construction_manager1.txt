Job Title: Senior Project Manager (Construction)

Company: LMN General Subcontractor

Description:
LMN General Subcontractor is seeking a motivated and experienced Senior Project Manager to join our team. The successful candidate will oversee all aspects of the construction project lifecycle, from initial planning to final closeout. You will be responsible for ensuring project milestones are met, budgets are maintained, and quality standards are upheld.

Responsibilities:

Develop comprehensive project plans, including scope, budget, schedule, and resource allocation.
Manage and coordinate with clients, architects, engineers, and other project stakeholders to ensure clear communication and alignment of expectations.
Select, negotiate with, and manage subcontractors and suppliers throughout the project lifecycle.
Regularly visit project sites to monitor progress, maintain quality control, and address any issues or concerns.
Identify and mitigate project risks, and implement corrective actions to prevent delays or budget overruns.
Ensure compliance with local, state, and federal regulations, as well as industry best practices.
Maintain accurate and up-to-date project documentation, including progress reports, change orders, and other relevant records.
Conduct project closeout procedures, including final inspections, punch lists, and warranty documentation.
Requirements:

Bachelor's degree in Construction Management, Civil Engineering, or a related field.
A minimum of 7 years of experience in construction project management, with a focus on general subcontractor management.
Demonstrated track record of successful project completion in the construction industry.
Proficiency in construction project management software, such as Procore, PlanGrid, or similar platforms.
Strong leadership, communication, and conflict resolution skills.
Ability to manage multiple projects simultaneously and adapt to changing priorities and deadlines.
Valid driver's license and ability to travel to project sites as needed.
Optional Requirements:

Advanced degree in Construction Management, Business Administration, or a related field.
Project Management Professional (PMP) or Associate Constructor (AC) certification.
Specialized experience in areas such as historic preservation, sustainable construction, or high-rise buildings.
Familiarity with Building Information Modeling (BIM) software and processes.
