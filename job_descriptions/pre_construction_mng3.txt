Job Title: Senior Preconstruction Manager

Company: STU General Subcontractor

Description:
STU General Subcontractor is looking for a highly experienced Senior Preconstruction Manager to lead our preconstruction team. The ideal candidate will have a strong background in construction estimating, scheduling, and project planning, ensuring our projects are set up for success from the outset.

Responsibilities:

Oversee the preconstruction process for multiple projects, including cost estimation, scheduling, and planning.
Review project plans, specifications, and contract documents to identify potential issues, risks, and opportunities for improvement.
Develop comprehensive project budgets, including detailed cost estimates, contingency plans, and value engineering options.
Collaborate with project managers, clients, architects, and other stakeholders to ensure project requirements are clearly defined and understood.
Manage and mentor preconstruction team members, providing guidance and support as needed.
Coordinate with subcontractors and suppliers to solicit and evaluate bids and proposals.
Prepare and present preconstruction documents, such as proposals, schedules, and budgets, to clients and stakeholders.
Stay current on industry trends, best practices, and market conditions to maintain a competitive edge.
Requirements:

Bachelor's degree in Construction Management, Civil Engineering, or a related field.
A minimum of 10 years of experience in the construction industry, with at least 5 years in a preconstruction or estimating role.
In-depth knowledge of construction processes, materials, and pricing strategies.
Proficiency in construction estimating and scheduling software, such as PlanSwift, ProEst, or Primavera P6.
Exceptional communication, leadership, and problem-solving skills.
Ability to manage multiple projects simultaneously and adapt to changing priorities.
Valid driver's license and ability to travel to project sites as required.
Optional Requirements:

Advanced degree in Construction Management, Business Administration, or a related field.
Professional certifications, such as the Certified Professional Estimator (CPE) or the Associate Constructor (AC).
Experience in specific construction sectors, such as healthcare, hospitality, or education.
