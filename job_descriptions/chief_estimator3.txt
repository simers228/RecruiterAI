Job Title: Lead Estimator

Company: MNO General Subcontractor

Description:
MNO General Subcontractor is seeking a highly skilled and knowledgeable Lead Estimator to direct our preconstruction efforts. The successful candidate will have a strong background in construction estimating, ensuring our bids are accurate, competitive, and set our projects up for success from the beginning.

Responsibilities:

Manage the estimating process for multiple projects, including cost estimation, scheduling, and planning.
Analyze project plans, specifications, and contract documents to identify potential issues, risks, and opportunities for improvement.
Develop comprehensive project budgets, including detailed cost estimates, contingency plans, and value engineering options.
Collaborate with project managers, clients, architects, and other stakeholders to ensure project requirements are clearly defined and understood.
Provide guidance and support to other estimating team members as needed.
Coordinate with subcontractors and suppliers to solicit and evaluate bids and proposals.
Prepare and present estimating documents, such as proposals, schedules, and budgets, to clients and stakeholders.
Stay current on industry trends, best practices, and market conditions to maintain a competitive edge.
Requirements:

Bachelor's degree in Construction Management, Civil Engineering, or a related field.
A minimum of 10 years of experience in the construction industry, with at least 5 years in an estimating role.
Comprehensive knowledge of construction processes, materials, and pricing strategies.
Proficiency in construction estimating and scheduling software, such as BidScreen XL, ProContractor, or Asta Powerproject.
Strong communication, leadership, and problem-solving skills.
Ability to manage multiple projects simultaneously and adapt to changing priorities.
Valid driver's license and ability to travel to project sites as required.
Optional Requirements:

Advanced degree in Construction Management, Business Administration, or a related field.
Professional certifications, such as the Certified Cost Professional (CCP) or the Project Management Professional (PMP).
Experience in specialized construction sectors, such as transportation, energy, or environmental projects.
